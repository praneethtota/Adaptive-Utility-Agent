====================================================================
  KEY FINDINGS
====================================================================

  1. Correct routing improves correctness by +12.5% over no routing
     (p=0.0086, Cohen's d=0.265).
     Routing to the correct domain specialist delivers quality gains
     equivalent to what domain fine-tuning provides, through prompt
     specialisation alone — no weight changes required.

  2. Mismatched routing reduces correctness by +17.5%
     (p=0.0004), confirming wrong-domain routing is
     actively harmful. Brier score worsens by +0.1323 — the model is overconfident
     on queries outside its specialist domain. This is Regime 2.

  3. VCG arbitration captures 84% of the oracle matched-routing
     gain (+10.5% vs +12.5%),
     despite imperfect routing accuracy (~82%). Confidence calibration
     also improves: Brier 0.1097 vs 0.1596 (generic).

  4. The gap between Arm B and Arm D (2.0%) is the
     remaining improvement available from higher routing accuracy.
     This is the target for the M1-M5 mitigations (§9.4.1): as routing
     accuracy rises toward 95%+, Arm D converges toward Arm B.

  SCOPE: These results demonstrate the routing and arbitration layer
  contributes measurable correctness improvement independent of model
  size. Arm B quality requires fine-tuned domain specialists; the
  specialist quality gains are supported by published benchmarks (cited
  in routing_results.json). Live Ollama validation is the next step.
